Urbanism must transcend modernist and capitalist logics—reimagining how cities are created, inhabited, and centred on their people.

Flooded Futures: Guayaquil

The project investigates from an urbanism perspective, integrating interdisciplinary dialogues to create a framework for scenario exploration in Guayaquil—embracing the process as a space for new forms of urban, architectural, and epistemic creation, while also integrating technology and visual narratives.

Superblock Cuenca

A strategy to reclaim public space and restructure mobility in Cuenca’s historic centre, drawing from the superblock model while adapting to the Andean context through ecological integration and human-scaled design.

BHF Berlin

A reinterpretation of Berlin’s urban block typology, merging historical references and small-scale planning to create a dense, permeable, and autonomous urban quarter at Bahnhof Zoo.
